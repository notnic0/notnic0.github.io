Favorite Clothing Item

In a thrift store haze where treasures dwell, Nico found a belt — a tale to tell. Tarnished buckle, leather worn , Frayed at edges, gently torn.

Once dismissed, now tightly tied, Around her waist with quiet pride. Not just fabric, loop and clasp, But memory’s thread in fashion’s grasp.

It held his jeans on autumn days, Danced in step through city’s maze. She wore it loud, he wore it true — The belt that thrift and style drew.

It saw late nights and early shows, Spilled coffee, concerts, highs and lows. A quiet rebel, it made no boast, Yet Nico loved that belt the most.

Not for brand, nor trend, nor wealth — But for its soul, its stitched-in stealth. Proof you don’t need gold to shine — Just a belt from Goodwill, and time.
